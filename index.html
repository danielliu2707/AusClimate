Temperature & Rainfall by Location

Regions along the coastline and southernmost areas of Australia tend to have cooler conditions compared to the central and northernmost regions, largely due to factors like the effect of sea breezes which keeps these areas cooler. The latitude of these areas also plays a significant role as coastline and southern regions are further from the equator, resulting in less direct sunlight.

The northernmost regions of Australia, including Queensland and the Northern Territory, receive the highest rainfall, cause by tropical and subtropical climates and their proximity to the equator.

These areas experience wet seasons during the summer months, driven by tropical cyclones and a plethora of thunderstorms. The combination of high levels of humidity and strong seasonal rainfall patterns results in significantly more precipitation.

Temperature & Rainfall by Month

Australia exhibits clear seasonal rainfall patterns, with significantly higher totals during early and later months of 2023, primarily driven by extreme rainfall in Queensland and the Northern Territory. These tropical regions are frequently affected by floods and cyclones which contribute to significant rainfall. While rainfall decreases in Queensland and the Northern Territory towards the middle of the year, other states generally experience higher rainfall during the middle months, due to the Southern Ocean, which brings moist air masses during winter.


There are clear seasonal temperature patterns in Australia, with all states following a similar trend. The warmest months typically occur towards the beginning and end months, while the coolest months fall inside the middle, aligning with Australia‚Äôs summer and winter. However, temperature variations can be more extreme in different states. The northern parts such as Queensland and the Northern Territory experience consistently high temperatures while southern states such as Victoria and New South Wales experience greater variations in temperature throughout the year.
